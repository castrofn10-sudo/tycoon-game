Rework the console/game market competition and annual award system to make it far more realistic, immersive, and competitive, inspired by serious game company tycoon gameplay.

STRICT RULES:
- Do not refactor architecture broadly
- Do not redesign unrelated UI
- Keep minimal structural changes
- Focus only on market competition, sales realism, rival strength, and annual awards logic

PROBLEMS TO FIX:
1. Weak products can still win major annual awards
2. Very low yearly sales (example: around 100 units) can become "best-selling console", which is unrealistic
3. Rival companies are not competitive enough
4. Office/research progression does not strongly gate product competitiveness
5. The market does not feel like a real war between companies

GOALS:
Create a more immersive and realistic game-company competition system where consoles and games compete in a meaningful market.

REQUIREMENTS:

1. Strengthen rival companies
- Rival companies must evolve more aggressively over time
- They should improve technology, innovation, finances, and market share
- Some rivals should have strong years and dominate the market
- Rivals should regularly threaten the player in both consoles and games

2. Make office and research progression matter more
- Product competitiveness must depend more strongly on:
  - office level
  - research unlocks
  - innovation
  - development quality
- Weak offices and poor research should heavily limit product quality and market success

3. Make console/game sales more realistic
- Increase realism of yearly market scale
- Ensure absolute sales numbers are meaningful for the era
- A product with extremely low sales must not be treated as market-dominating
- Sales ranking should reflect actual competition against rivals

4. Rework annual awards
- Annual awards must compare all products from all companies
- Add proper ranking: 1st, 2nd, 3rd
- The player should not win by default
- Low-quality or low-sales products must not win major awards
- Add minimum thresholds for "best-selling" awards
- If no product reaches a credible threshold, do not grant a major winner title

5. Add better award evaluation logic
Use a weighted score combining:
- quality
- innovation
- sales
- reputation
- market impact
- small randomness factor

6. Add realistic thresholds
- Prevent tiny sales numbers from winning “Console of the Year” or “Best-Selling Console”
- Add era-aware scaling if needed
- Example: expected market size in 1972 should still make 100 units feel tiny, not dominant

7. Make the market feel like a true company war
- Stronger rivalry for games and consoles
- Real competition for yearly rankings
- More variation in winners between years
- Some years the player should win nothing
- Some years player should place 2nd or 3rd
- Some years rivals should fully dominate

8. Keep current save/load compatibility
- Persist any new ranking/award fields safely

9. Explain clearly after changes:
- which files were changed
- how rival competition was improved
- how office/research now affects competitiveness
- how annual award ranking now works
- what thresholds were added to prevent unrealistic winners
