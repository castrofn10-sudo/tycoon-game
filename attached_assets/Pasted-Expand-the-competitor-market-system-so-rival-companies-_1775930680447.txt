Expand the competitor market system so rival companies grow, decline, fail, and become acquirable over time.

Requirements:

1. Make rival companies evolve every month in a realistic way.
Each company should dynamically update:
- cash
- monthly revenue
- monthly profit/loss
- reputation
- innovation
- market share
- market value

2. Add a market value system.
Each rival company must have a calculated market value based on:
- cash
- annual revenue
- reputation
- innovation
- market share

Use a reasonable formula and keep it balanced.

3. Make rivals able to grow or decline.
Their finances and strength should change over time based on:
- product quality
- launches
- innovation
- market trends
- attacks/news
- reputation
- market share

4. Add company crisis and bankruptcy progression.
If a company stays financially weak for too long:
- enter crisis state
- lose value
- risk bankruptcy
- eventually fail if not recovered

5. Add acquisition system.
If the player has enough money, they should be able to attempt to buy a company.
Acquisition logic should consider:
- target company market value
- target company financial health
- target company aggressiveness
- player money
- player reputation
- random negotiation factor

6. Add the ability to buy bankrupt companies at a discount.
When a company fails:
- player can buy it or its assets
- acquisition price should be lower than full market value
- buying should affect market share and maybe innovation/reputation

7. Allow all companies in the market to potentially become purchasable if:
- player has enough money
- target company is willing to sell
- conditions are met

8. Keep it balanced.
- Large healthy companies should not sell easily
- Weak companies should be more open to acquisition
- Very strong player companies should be able to dominate the market over time

9. Add UI support where appropriate.
On the market/company screen, show:
- market value
- financial health
- acquisition availability
- estimated buyout cost
- status (healthy / pressured / crisis / bankrupt)

10. Do not break existing save/load systems.
Persist all new company fields safely.

11. Explain clearly:
- which files were changed
- what new fields were added to companies
- how market value is calculated
- how acquisition and bankruptcy work
